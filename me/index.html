Color


When I was in grade school I would always play slug-bug with my much older, much stronger brother. I would yell "RED", and hit him with as much force that I could muster, which to him was like a feather fell on him. This would always cause my parents to just laugh and laugh. Well come to find out, they were NOT laughing at that sweet bloody piece of revenge I was enacting on my brother, but instead were actually laughing at the me calling an orange car red. My brother did not notice the difference either. Well come to find out, like my brother I was color blind. On top of that, BAM, I find out I am also dyslexic, like my father. It all made since, I wasn't completely dumb, just my genes were.

On a more serious note, these were things I never knew made me different, if anything they only slowed me down and caused me to have to explain to new people why my clothes never match. Honestly though, I do not even want to imagine this world living without sight or the ability to hear. Disabilities are the things for which the world is not designed. So when I set out to create or build something new, I design it to be accommodating to everyone. Whether that be applications that take full advantage of accessibility features or designing my home so that everyone can feel safe and be able to use everything in it without boundaries. Good design is built for everyone, because what are you designing it for, if not for everyone.



Family


Miriam Primrose Miller was my grandmother on my fathers side. She was a mother to 3 daughters and 5 sons, grandmother to 30+ grandkids, and great-grandmother to 40+ great-grandchildren. She was a complete and ethereal LEGEND. One of the sweetest women I have ever know. She was the one who taught me the importance of family and taking care of others. A women that would give her house to anyone who needed a roof for the night or feed you the little food she had in her fridge because she “didn’t need it”. She ended up living the last few years of her life in my parents home where we got to give back some of the love and care she gave so many throughout her 90 years. I carry these lessons of love in my heart everyday. Every moment, I try to treat the people around me like family, because if she can give so much with so little resources, we all can.



Lost


Growing up a chunky kid teaches you lots of lessons in character. I knew no matter how many times I was bullied and put down for having bigger boobs then probably most of the girls in my grade at the time, that one day I would lose the weight. It was a matter of time before I came to terms with myself. I knew I had it in me to overcome the weight. I also knew it would require an enormous change in my life and that was not an easy self confrontation. Sophomore year I decided to take that fat-ole bull by the horns.

I worked out an hour everyday and counted every calorie till I retrained my mind to not eat like I was about to go on a diet and needed one last good cheat meal for the 20 day in a row. After tons of hard work I lost about 2 pounds per week for an entire year. 100 pounds later, I went from 270 pounds all the way down to 168. This wasn’t the end of my journey though, in fact that was the easy part. Now I Had to deal with all of self-esteem issues; when you go from being large your whole life to all the sudden looking like pre-serum Captain America, that image is unprecedented for your inward self. I am proud today to finally have fought for self confidence and won. After years of people putting me down, I finally built myself up, that is something that emboldened my heart and those people only made me stronger for it, and for that I am grateful, but also don't be a bully, it's no good mate.



Pride

On this day I decided to finally tell my parents that I am gay, two weeks later I came out to my siblings, and now I live everyday proud of the man I am today. I can live in a world where I do not have to hide what is underneath, and for that I am so thankful. Fairness and equality make up so much of my character, and I truly believe this world wasn’t meant for any one soul, but everyone. Being together as one humanity is what makes us strong, this earth is one we all walk on with no choice in how the world comes at us, but all the choice in how we treat the world.

I looked through the myriad of different programs they had, knowing that I wanted to do something toying with computers. Frankly because I knew I was good enough with them to be called a "computer guru" anytime my family needed technical support and thats gotta count for something (right?). Anyways, when I came across their program called “Computer Information Science (CIS)” I thought that sounded weirdly fun and I pulled the trigger almost instantly (and by instantly I meant Junior year my mom didn't want me to miss out on my glorious high school years. So I waited a year and rebranded my aproach. The powerpoint I made was a success).

First class of the semester was 'Prelude to Problem Solving' with, my teacher, Kirsten Markley. Mrs. Markley was a phenomenal teacher, and in all honesty I do not know if I would still be programming today if she had not taught that class. She sparked my curse for love of software development. I have been programming for roughly 5 years now, with one more year of college left before I graduate with a Bachelors in Computer Science. This is a love and a super power like no other and I am so proud of path it has lead me down.
