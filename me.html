Color


When I was in grade school I had this art teacher, very impatient to be teaching young kids or even art for that matter. She would tell us to grab a color or draw a sky and always just get so frustrated when I grabbed the wrong color. Some times I would grab the color she needed or drew the correct color of my subject, but every so often I would be off. It was not consistent which is why I think it perturbed her so much. Well long down the line, probably way too long, I discovered that like my brother I was color blind. Also come to find out I am also dyslexic, like my father. I remember my 'speed math’ problems taking me 6x as long as the other kids, and when my brother and I would play slug-bug my parents would chuckle when we would call a blue car purple.

These were things I never knew made me different. These things were never disabilities for me, if anything they just slow me down a tad and cause some hick-ups now and again, also are great for comedy, but to imagine living life without sight or the ability to hear. Those are the things for which the world is not designed. So when I set to create or build something, I design it to be accommodating to everyone. Whether that be applications that take full advantage of accessibility features or decorating my home to where everyone can feel safe and be able to use everything in it without boundaries. Good design is built for everyone, because why are you designing something for, if everyone cannot use it.



Family


Miriam Primrose Miller was my grandmother on my fathers side. She was a mother to 3 daughters and 5 sons, grandmother to 30+ grandkids, and great-grandmother to 40+ great-grandchildren. She was a complete LEGEND. One of the sweetest ladies I have ever know. She was the one who taught me the importance of family and taking care of others. She would give her house to anyone homeless who needed a roof for the night or feed you the little food she had in her fridge because she “didn’t need it”. She ended up living the last few years of her life in my parents home where we got to give back some of the love and care she gave so many throughout her 90 years. I carry those lessons of love in my heart everyday. Everyday I try to treat the people around me like I would family; we are all spinning on this rock together and if we treat others with love, that’s when we’re most equip to defeat hate.



Lost


Growing up a chunky kid teaches you lots of lessons in character: how to deal with bullies, how to deal with low self confidence, and the only person’s opinion about you that matters is the opinion of yourself. I always grew up thinking one day I would lose the weight and even when picturing my future I always saw myself skinny. It was only a matter of time before I came to terms with myself. It would require an enormous change in my life and that was not an easy self confrontation. Sophomore year I decided that it was time for that change.

I worked out about an hour everyday and counted calories till I retrained my mind to eat healthy. After tons of hard work I lost about 2 pounds a week for a year. 100 pounds later, I went from 270 pounds all the way down to 168. That wasn’t the end of my journey though. I dealt with lots of self confidence issues; you can go from being large your whole life to all the sudden skinny, its unprecedented for your inward self. I am proud today to finally have fought for self confidence and won. After years of people putting me down, I finally build myself up, that is something that emboldened my heart and those people only made me stronger for it, and for that I am grateful.



Pride


September 16, 2017; After dealing with so much pain in my heart and soul, I had realize it was time to finally be me. On this day I decided to finally tell my parents that I am gay, two weeks later I came out to my siblings, and now I live everyday proud of who I am. I can live in a world where I do not have to hide what is underneath, and for that I am so grateful. Through all this, I try to live every day putting in the work to support everyone around me, no matter what they look like or how they speak or any way their life might be different from mine or the small world I grew up in. Fairness and equality make up so much of my life and behavior, I believe this world wasn’t meant for any one soul, but everyone. Being together as one humanity is what makes us strong, this earth is one we all walk on with no choice in how the world comes at us, but all the choice in how we treat the world.



Programming


Senior year of high school I got offered the opportunity to go to the local community college in the mornings and attend classes for free through my high school. I looked through the different programs they had, know I wanted to do something with computers and I came across a program called “Computer Information Science (CIS)” so I said that sounds weirdly fun and pulled the trigger. First class of the semester was Prelude to Problem Solving with my teacher, Kirsten Markley. Mrs. Markley was a phenomenal programming teacher, and in all honesty I do not know if I would still be programming today if she had not taught that class, and began my love of software development. Since then I have been programming for roughly 5 years now and I have one more year of college left before I graduate with a Bachelors in Computer Science.

I have quite a love for all programming and problem solving. From web development to desktop applications my programming skills have expanded drastically from those first days of writing “Hello World” to the console with python. I try to learn new things everyday, from just starting to learning javascript, to freshening up my html/css skills with this very website. Programming I just a fantastic way for me to sharpen my brain and keep my creative side satisfied.
